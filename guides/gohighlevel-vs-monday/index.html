What Monday.com Does Well

Monday.com is one of the most popular work management platforms in the world, and for good reason. It is genuinely excellent at what it was originally built to do: helping teams organize, track, and collaborate on projects and workflows.

Visual Boards and Customizable Views

Monday's signature feature is its board system. Every workflow gets a board. Every board has columns you can customize — status, date, person, number, formula, file, whatever your workflow requires. You can view the same data as a table, a Kanban board, a timeline (Gantt chart), a calendar, a chart, or a map. This flexibility is genuinely impressive. If you can think of a way to visualize work, Monday probably supports it.

For teams managing complex projects with multiple stakeholders, dependencies, and deadlines, this level of visual customization is a real advantage. You can see at a glance what is on track, what is behind, and who is responsible for what.

Team Collaboration

Monday was built for teams. Every item on a board can have comments, file attachments, mentions, and activity logs. Updates flow through a central feed so everyone stays informed without endless email threads. Task assignments, workload views, and team dashboards make it easy to manage a group without constant meetings.

Flexibility Across Use Cases

Monday is a horizontal platform. People use it for project management, HR onboarding, content calendars, inventory tracking, event planning, and dozens of other use cases. The template library is extensive — you can spin up a new board for almost any workflow in minutes. For teams that manage diverse operational processes, this adaptability is valuable.

Monday CRM vs Monday Work Management — They Are Separate Products

This is the most important thing to understand about Monday.com in the context of this comparison: Monday Work Management and Monday CRM are separate products with separate pricing.

Monday Work Management is the project management tool most people know. It handles tasks, timelines, collaboration, and operational workflows. When someone says "I use Monday," they are almost always talking about this product.

Monday CRM is a newer, dedicated sales product that Monday.com launched to compete in the CRM market. It includes deal pipelines, contact management, email tracking, activity logging, sales forecasting, and some automation features. It is built on the same visual board infrastructure as Work Management, which gives it a familiar feel if you already use Monday for projects.

The confusion happens when solo founders sign up for Monday thinking they are getting both project management and a full CRM in one subscription. The reality is more nuanced. Monday CRM is designed as a sales pipeline tool, not a marketing automation platform. It tracks deals. It does not generate leads. It logs emails. It does not run email campaigns. It manages contacts. It does not build landing pages, send SMS sequences, or automate review requests.

Pricing: What You Actually Pay

Pricing is where this comparison gets really interesting for solo founders, because the sticker price on Monday CRM is deceptively low.

Monday CRM Pricing

  • Free: Up to 2 seats. Very limited — 1,000 contacts, no email tracking, no automations, no integrations. Fine for testing, not usable for a real business.
  • Basic — $12/seat/month: Unlimited contacts, unlimited pipelines, and deal management. But still no automations, no email templates, and limited integrations. You are essentially getting a visual spreadsheet for your deals.
  • Standard — $17/seat/month: This is where Monday CRM becomes functional. You get email integration with tracking, merge fields, custom automations (250 actions per month), quotes, and invoices. For most solo founders, this is the minimum viable plan.
  • Pro — $28/seat/month: Email templates, mass emails, sales forecasting, advanced analytics, 25,000 automation actions per month. This is the plan for teams that want serious CRM functionality.

Important detail: Monday CRM requires a minimum of 3 seats on paid plans. So the real starting cost on the Standard plan is closer to $51 per month, even if you are a team of one. And at $51 per month, you have a CRM — that is it. You still need email marketing ($20-50/month), SMS ($25-50/month), a landing page builder ($29-79/month), a booking tool ($8-15/month), and a review platform ($50-150/month). The total stack cost to match GHL easily reaches $200 to $400 per month.

GoHighLevel Pricing

  • Starter — $97/month: CRM with unlimited contacts, email marketing, SMS (usage-based messaging costs apply), funnel and landing page builder, booking calendar, forms, workflow automation, reputation management. One sub-account (your business). Unlimited users. Everything a solo founder needs.
  • Unlimited — $297/month: Everything in Starter plus unlimited sub-accounts, API access, and white-labeling. This is for agency owners who want to resell the platform to clients. Most solo founders do not need this.

For a solo founder, GHL at $97 per month replaces what would cost $200 to $400 per month in Monday CRM plus add-on tools. And you eliminate the complexity of managing multiple subscriptions, multiple logins, and multiple integration points that can break.

When to Pick Monday.com

Monday.com is the right choice in specific scenarios. Be honest about whether these describe your situation.

You need project management first, CRM second. If the primary challenge in your business is managing complex projects with multiple team members, dependencies, timelines, and deliverables — and you also want a lightweight CRM to track deals on the side — Monday makes sense. You get world-class project management with a functional CRM that uses the same interface. The CRM is not the star of the show, but it does not need to be if project management is your main pain point.

Your team manages complex internal workflows. If you have a team of 5, 10, or 20 people working across departments and you need visibility into who is doing what, when things are due, and where bottlenecks are forming, Monday's collaboration features are hard to beat. The workload views, dashboard widgets, and cross-board automations are built for teams that need coordination at scale.

You already have a marketing stack you are happy with. If you are already using Mailchimp, Calendly, and a landing page builder and those tools work for you, Monday CRM slots in as a deal-tracking layer without disrupting your setup.

You do not need SMS, funnels, or review automation. If your sales process is primarily email-based and your clients come through referrals, Monday CRM covers your needs without the extra functionality you would be paying for with GHL.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Monday.com a real CRM or just a project management tool?

Monday.com offers two separate products: Monday Work Management (the project management tool most people know) and Monday CRM (a dedicated sales pipeline product). Monday CRM includes deal tracking, contact management, and basic email integration. However, it lacks built-in SMS, landing pages, funnels, and review automation that platforms like GoHighLevel include natively.

Can GoHighLevel replace Monday.com for project management?

No. GoHighLevel is not a project management tool. It does not have task boards, Gantt charts, workload views, or team collaboration features for managing internal projects. If you need project management alongside your CRM, you would need to keep a separate tool for that. GHL replaces your marketing and sales stack, not your project management stack.

Which is cheaper for a solo founder — GoHighLevel or Monday CRM?

GHL at $97 per month includes CRM, email marketing, SMS, funnels, landing pages, booking calendars, review management, and workflow automation with unlimited users. Monday CRM starts free for up to 2 users, but the free tier is extremely limited. To get meaningful automation and integrations, you need the Standard plan at $17 per seat per month — and you still need to buy separate tools for SMS, landing pages, funnels, and review management. For a solo founder who needs marketing and sales automation, GHL is typically cheaper than Monday CRM plus the additional tools required to match its functionality.

Does Monday CRM have email marketing and SMS?

Monday CRM has basic email tracking and templates for one-to-one sales emails, but it does not have a built-in email campaign builder for broadcast marketing or drip sequences. It has no native SMS capability at all. You would need to integrate third-party tools like Mailchimp for email campaigns and a separate SMS platform, adding cost and complexity.

Can I migrate from Monday CRM to GoHighLevel?

Yes. GHL supports CSV import for contacts and deals. You can export your Monday CRM data and import it into GHL. Pipeline stages and custom fields need to be recreated manually, but the contact and deal data transfers cleanly. Most solo founders complete the migration in a single focused afternoon.
