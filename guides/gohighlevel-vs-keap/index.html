GoHighLevel vs Keap: CRM & Marketing Platform Comparison

Compare GoHighLevel and Keap (formerly Infusionsoft) for CRM and marketing automation. Pricing, features, and which all-in-one platform wins for agencies.

Choosing an all-in-one CRM and marketing platform is one of the most consequential decisions for agencies and service businesses. GoHighLevel and Keap (formerly Infusionsoft) both promise to consolidate your tech stack‚Äîbut they serve very different needs. One is built for the agency model. The other is a legacy player trying to stay relevant. Let's break down which one deserves your business.

‚ö° GoHighLevel Strengths

GoHighLevel was built specifically for agencies who want to resell marketing services. The white-label capability is unmatched‚Äîyou can fully rebrand the platform as your own software and sell it to clients. This creates a recurring revenue stream beyond traditional retainer work.

Unlimited sub-accounts on the Agency Unlimited plan ($297/mo) is a game-changer. Whether you have 5 clients or 500, the price stays the same. Compare that to per-seat pricing models that punish growth.

The feature density is staggering: CRM, email marketing, SMS campaigns, phone system, funnel builder, website builder, appointment scheduling, membership sites, course hosting, reputation management, and more. Tools that would cost $500-1000/month separately are bundled in one platform.

GoHighLevel is actively developed with new features shipping constantly. The community is massive and engaged. Agencies share templates, workflows, and strategies freely. The ecosystem creates compounding value.

üîß Keap Strengths

Keap (rebranded from the infamous Infusionsoft) has 20+ years of CRM experience. For solopreneurs and small teams who need proven email automation and contact management, Keap delivers reliability.

The campaign builder, while complex, offers sophisticated automation logic. If you've invested time mastering Keap's system, the sequences can be genuinely powerful for nurturing leads over time.

E-commerce and payment processing are well-integrated. For businesses selling products or services directly (not agencies), Keap handles quotes, invoices, and recurring billing reasonably well.

Keap has a large certified partner network. If you want someone else to set up your CRM, you'll find Keap consultants. Whether that's a strength or a crutch depends on your perspective.

‚öñÔ∏è Key Differences

Pricing Philosophy: GoHighLevel offers unlimited contacts and (on higher tiers) unlimited sub-accounts. Keap charges based on contacts‚Äîyou pay more as you grow. At scale, GHL costs a fraction of Keap.

Target User: GoHighLevel is built for agencies who serve multiple clients and want to productize their services. Keap targets single businesses managing their own marketing. Different problems, different solutions.

Modern vs. Legacy: GoHighLevel launched in 2018 and iterates rapidly. Keap carries 20 years of technical debt from the Infusionsoft era. The UX difference is immediately obvious‚ÄîGHL feels modern; Keap feels dated.

White-Label Revenue: Only GoHighLevel lets you rebrand and resell the platform. Agencies can charge $297-497/month per client for "their" software while paying a flat fee to GHL. Keap doesn't offer this model.

Feature Scope: GoHighLevel consolidates more tools (funnels, SMS, calling, courses). Keap focuses on core CRM and email. If you need a true all-in-one, GHL delivers more value.

üéØ The Verdict

Choose GoHighLevel if: You're an agency or consultant serving multiple clients. You want white-label SaaS revenue. You need funnels, SMS, and calling built-in. You want unlimited scaling without per-contact pricing.

Choose Keap if: You're a single small business (not an agency). You already use Keap and migration cost is prohibitive. You need deep e-commerce integration. You prefer working with certified implementation partners.

For most agencies and service businesses in 2025, GoHighLevel is the clear winner. The pricing model, white-label capability, and feature set are purpose-built for the agency business model. Keap served its era well, but that era has passed.
