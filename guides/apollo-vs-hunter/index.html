Apollo.io vs Hunter: B2B Email Finder Showdown

Compare Apollo.io and Hunter.io for B2B prospecting. Email finding accuracy, enrichment features, pricing, and which platform wins.

Finding accurate B2B email addresses is the foundation of successful outreach. Apollo.io and Hunter.io both promise to solve this problem—but with fundamentally different approaches. Apollo is a full sales intelligence platform with email finding built in. Hunter is a focused email finder and verifier. Understanding the difference helps you pick the right tool for your workflow.

🚀 Apollo.io Strengths

Apollo is a complete sales intelligence and engagement platform. Yes, it finds emails—but it also provides phone numbers, company data, tech stack information, funding history, and buying intent signals. You're not just getting contact info; you're getting context to personalize outreach.

The built-in sequencing means you can prospect, enrich, and launch campaigns without leaving Apollo. Find 100 marketing directors at SaaS companies, add them to a 5-step email sequence, and track engagement—all in one tool. This consolidation saves money and reduces workflow friction.

Apollo's filters are incredibly granular. Search by job title, seniority, department, company size, industry, technologies used, recent funding, and more. Building hyper-targeted lists for ABM campaigns is straightforward.

The free tier is generous: 10,000 email credits/month and limited sequencing. For bootstrapped founders testing outbound, Apollo offers real value at $0.

🎯 Hunter.io Strengths

Hunter does one thing exceptionally well: find and verify email addresses. The domain search instantly shows all emails associated with a company domain. The email finder takes a name and company, returning the most likely email format. The verifier checks if an address is deliverable.

For users who already have a CRM and email platform, Hunter slots in as a specialized enrichment tool. It's lightweight, fast, and doesn't try to replace your existing stack—it enhances it.

Hunter's Chrome extension is beloved for quick lookups. Visit any website, click the extension, and see associated email addresses instantly. For journalists, recruiters, and bizdev folks doing ad-hoc research, this speed matters.

The API is clean and well-documented. Developers love integrating Hunter into custom workflows, lead enrichment pipelines, and CRM automations. The simplicity of "give domain, get emails" is refreshing.

🎯 Why This Comparison Matters

Choosing a B2B prospecting tool determines outreach ROI. Apollo includes sequencing, while Hunter is pure lookup. For a founder running cold email campaigns, Apollo costs $49/mo and handles finding + sequencing. Hunter costs $99/mo and requires Outreach or similar ($100+/mo). Choosing wrong means paying for gaps or overpaying for features you don't need.

🔍 Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

Email Data Quality & Volume

Apollo: 250M+ contacts, verified database, continuous updates. Email accuracy around 95% with verification system. Domain data includes job titles, departments, seniority levels. Comprehensive contact enrichment included.

Hunter: 100M+ verified emails with industry-leading verification. Email accuracy slightly higher on verified set (96-98%). Focuses on email accuracy over additional data points. Lightweight data focused on contact info.

Prospecting Workflow

Apollo: Find emails → add to list → auto-sequence → track engagement. All in one platform. Built-in engagement tracking shows opens, clicks, replies. You can personalize sequences based on company data.

Hunter: Find emails → export to CRM → set up sequence in your email tool. Requires switching between tools. Better if you already have email/CRM infrastructure.

Research & Enrichment

Apollo: Shows company financials, tech stack, employee count, funding rounds, recent company updates. Detailed contact information (phone, title, seniority). You can filter lists by buying signals (recent funding, new hires, tech stack changes).

Hunter: Shows associated company name and email count at that domain. Limited enrichment beyond email. API simple: domain + name = email. Doesn't try to do research.

Integration & Automation

Apollo: Native CRM, less need for integrations. Built-in workflows handle most automation. API available for custom development.

Hunter: Excellent API, integrates anywhere. Zapier integration connects Hunter to any workflow. Better for augmenting existing tools than replacing them.

🔗 Integration Ecosystem

Apollo's Native Integration

Apollo integrates with Slack, Gmail, HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, and others. The Chrome extension works on LinkedIn, Gmail, and any website. The platform tries to be self-contained, reducing need for external integrations.

Hunter's API-First Approach

Hunter has clean REST API documentation. Zapier integration lets you trigger actions on emails found (add to Slack, create Salesforce contact, etc.). Better for custom workflows and tool-agnostic teams.

Integration Decision

Choose Apollo if: You want one tool for email finding + sequencing + CRM. You're starting fresh. Integration simplicity matters.

Choose Hunter if: You have existing CRM/email tools. You want email lookup to integrate into custom workflows. Flexibility and specialization matter.

⚖️ Key Differences

Scope: Apollo is an all-in-one platform; Hunter is a specialized tool. If you need CRM, sequencing, and prospecting together, Apollo wins. If you just need email finding alongside existing tools, Hunter is simpler.

Data Depth: Apollo provides extensive company and contact enrichment. Hunter focuses on email addresses. For research-heavy sales processes, Apollo's additional data points are valuable.

Workflow: Apollo wants to be your primary sales tool. Hunter integrates into whatever workflow you already have. Your preference depends on whether you want consolidation or best-of-breed flexibility.

Accuracy: Both have strong verification, but Apollo's larger database often finds more contacts per company. Hunter's confidence scores help you gauge deliverability risk.

🎯 The Verdict

Choose Apollo if: You want an all-in-one sales platform. You need company intelligence beyond just emails. You're building targeted lists and running sequences from the same tool.

Choose Hunter if: You already have a CRM and email tool. You need quick, reliable email lookups. You prefer specialized tools over platforms.
