Best Cold Email Tool for Agencies (2025)

We tested the top cold email platforms to find which actually works for agencies managing multiple clients at scale.

Running cold email for an agency is a different beast than running it for a single company. You need to manage multiple clients, maintain separate sending reputations, scale without destroying deliverability, and ideally provide white-label reporting. Most cold email tools are built for individual users‚Äînot agencies running campaigns for 10, 20, or 50+ clients.

We spent months testing the leading platforms specifically for agency use cases. Here's what we found.

What Agencies Actually Need

Before diving into tools, let's clarify what separates agency-grade cold email software from tools built for individual founders:

  • Unlimited sending accounts ‚Äî You need dozens or hundreds of email accounts across clients
  • Built-in email warmup ‚Äî Without this, new accounts hit spam immediately
  • Client workspace separation ‚Äî Keep campaigns, contacts, and data isolated
  • Deliverability infrastructure ‚Äî The platform should actively protect your sender reputation
  • White-label reporting ‚Äî Share results with clients under your brand
  • Team permissions ‚Äî Give clients or team members access without exposing everything

1. Instantly
The agency gold standard for cold email at scale
Editor's Pick

Instantly has become the go-to platform for lead gen agencies, and for good reason. It's built from the ground up for high-volume cold email with deliverability as the core focus.

The unlimited email accounts are the killer feature. Most competitors charge per inbox or cap you at a certain number. Instantly lets you connect as many sending accounts as you need‚Äîcritical when each client might have 5-10 inboxes in rotation.

Their warmup network is massive (200k+ accounts), which means your emails get warmed up by real users on real domains. Combined with their sending rotation and smart throttling, you're maximizing deliverability automatically.

Pros
  • Unlimited email accounts on all plans
  • Best-in-class warmup network
  • Campaign analytics built for agencies
  • Workspace separation for clients
  • Smart sending rotation & throttling
  • Excellent deliverability out of the box
Cons
  • No built-in CRM (use with other tools)
  • Lead database costs extra
  • Learning curve for advanced features
Pricing: Growth plan at $37/mo (unlimited accounts, 5,000 leads). Hypergrowth at $97/mo for agencies (25,000 leads + advanced analytics). Enterprise pricing for high-volume agencies.

2. Smartlead
Strong Instantly alternative with unique features
#2

Smartlead is the closest competitor to Instantly and gives them a real run for their money. They also offer unlimited email accounts and focus heavily on deliverability.

What sets Smartlead apart is their unified inbox (manage replies across all accounts in one place) and their white-label capabilities. If you want to fully rebrand the platform for your clients, Smartlead makes this easier than most.

The API is also more robust, making it better for agencies that want to build custom integrations or automations around their cold email workflows.

Pros
  • Unlimited email accounts
  • Strong white-label options
  • Unified inbox for all replies
  • Good API for custom integrations
  • Auto-rotating sending
Cons
  • Interface less polished than Instantly
  • Warmup network smaller
  • Support can be slow
Pricing: Basic at $39/mo (unlimited accounts, 2,000 leads). Pro at $94/mo (30,000 leads). Custom pricing for white-label.
3. Lemlist
Best for personalized, creative campaigns
#3

Lemlist pioneered personalized images and videos in cold email. If your agency's differentiator is highly creative, personalized outreach, Lemlist gives you tools others don't.

The personalization features are genuinely impressive‚Äîdynamic images with the prospect's name, company logo, or custom variables embedded. This can significantly boost reply rates for the right campaigns.

However, Lemlist's pricing model charges per user/seat, which gets expensive fast for agencies. The lack of unlimited sending accounts is a real limitation at scale.

Pros
  • Unique image/video personalization
  • Beautiful email templates
  • Good deliverability tools
  • Multi-channel sequences (email + LinkedIn)
Cons
  • Per-seat pricing hurts agencies
  • Limited email accounts per plan
  • More expensive at scale
  • Less suited for pure volume plays
Pricing: Email Starter at $39/mo (1 sending email). Email Pro at $69/mo (3 sending emails). Enterprise for teams.
4. Apollo.io
Best for combined prospecting + outreach
#4

Apollo is a hybrid tool‚Äîpart data provider, part cold email platform. If you're currently paying separately for lead data and email sending, Apollo's all-in-one approach might save money.

Their B2B database of 275M+ contacts is legitimately useful. You can find leads and email them from the same platform without exporting/importing. For agencies that do their own prospecting, this streamlines the workflow.

The downside: Apollo's email features are good but not best-in-class. It's a "jack of all trades" situation. Agencies prioritizing pure cold email performance usually pair a dedicated data tool with Instantly.

Pros
  • Massive B2B contact database included
  • Prospecting + outreach in one tool
  • Chrome extension for LinkedIn
  • Good CRM features built-in
  • Generous free tier
Cons
  • Email features not as advanced
  • Warmup not as robust
  • Per-seat pricing model
  • Better for sales teams than agencies
Pricing: Free tier available. Basic at $49/user/mo. Professional at $99/user/mo with more credits.
5. Woodpecker
Reliable veteran, better for smaller agencies
#5

Woodpecker has been in the cold email game since 2015. It's mature, stable, and has a dedicated agency version with client management features.

The Agency panel lets you manage multiple clients from one dashboard, with separate campaigns and settings for each. It's well-designed for the agency workflow.

However, Woodpecker's pricing scales per contacted prospect, which can get expensive at high volumes. Their feature set also hasn't evolved as quickly as newer competitors like Instantly.

Pros
  • Dedicated Agency panel
  • Mature, stable platform
  • Good automation rules
  • Solid deliverability
  • Integrates with most CRMs
Cons
  • Per-prospect pricing adds up
  • Interface feels dated
  • Fewer sending accounts than competitors
  • Innovation has slowed
Pricing: Starts at $29/mo per slot. Agency pricing available on request.
