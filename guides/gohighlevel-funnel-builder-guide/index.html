Types of Funnels You Can Build

Before you open the builder, you need to know what kind of funnel matches your business model. GHL supports all of the common funnel types, and choosing the right one determines everything about your page structure, your copy, and your automation.

Opt-in / lead magnet funnel. The most common starting point. Page one offers a free resource — guide, checklist, template — in exchange for an email. Page two delivers the resource and introduces your core offer. If you are building your first funnel, start here.

Webinar registration funnel. Page one sells the webinar. Page two confirms the registration. Post-webinar automation pushes toward your paid offer. Works well for coaches, consultants, and course creators who sell through education.

Sales page funnel. Long-form sales page with objection handling, social proof, and payment collection via Stripe. Add an upsell page after purchase to increase average order value. No separate checkout tool required.

Booking funnel. Page one pre-sells the value of a discovery call. Page two embeds GHL's built-in calendar for direct booking. A pre-call questionnaire qualifies leads before they reach your calendar.

Tripwire funnel. A low-ticket offer ($7 to $47) that converts cold visitors into paying customers. Someone who has paid you even a small amount is dramatically more likely to buy your core offer later.

Application funnel. For high-ticket services. Page one sells the value of applying. Page two collects information through a multi-step form. Qualified applicants get routed to a booking page. Non-qualifying applicants get redirected to an alternative resource.

Step-by-Step: Building Your First Funnel in GoHighLevel

This walkthrough uses an opt-in funnel as the example because it is the most universally useful starting point. The steps translate directly to any other funnel type — only the copy and page structure change.

Step 1: Create Your Funnel

Inside your GHL account, navigate to Sites and then Funnels. Click "Create New Funnel." You will see two options: start from a template or start from scratch. If this is your first funnel, start with a template. GHL's template library includes pre-built opt-in funnels with proven layouts. Pick one that matches your style, and you will have a working structure in seconds that you can customize.

Name your funnel something descriptive — "Lead Magnet Funnel - [Your Resource Name]" — so you can identify it later when you have multiple funnels running.

Step 2: Set Up Your Funnel Steps

A basic opt-in funnel needs two steps: a landing page (where visitors opt in) and a thank-you page (where they receive the resource and see your next offer). Click "Add New Step" to add pages. For each step, you define the page name, the URL path, and whether it is the entry point of the funnel. Keep URL paths clean and readable — /free-guide is better than /page-1-v3-final.

Step 3: Edit Your Landing Page

Click on the landing page step to open the drag-and-drop editor. This is where you build the actual page your visitors will see. The editor works in rows and columns. You add a row, define its column structure (full width, two columns, three columns), and then drag elements into each column.

For an opt-in page, the essential elements are:

  • A headline that communicates the specific outcome of your lead magnet. Not "Download Our Free Guide" but "The 5-Step System That Books 15 Calls Per Week on Autopilot."
  • A sub-headline that adds context. Who is this for, and what will they get? "Built for solo consultants. No ads. No cold outreach. Free PDF."
  • A hero image or mockup of the resource to make it tangible.
  • 3 to 5 bullet points highlighting what the reader will learn or gain.
  • An opt-in form asking for name and email (fewer fields = higher conversion).
  • A call-to-action button with action-oriented text — "Get the Free Guide" beats "Submit."

Toggle to mobile view and check every element. Over 60% of your traffic will arrive on phones, so mobile testing is not optional.

Step 4: Connect Your Form to the CRM and Pipeline

This is where GHL's integrated approach pays off. Click on your form element, then configure what happens when someone submits. In the form settings:

  • Map form fields to contact fields. First name goes to First Name. Email goes to Email. This creates or updates the contact in your CRM automatically.
  • Apply tags. Add a tag like "Lead Magnet - [Resource Name]" so you can segment these contacts later for targeted campaigns.
  • Add to pipeline. Assign new leads to a specific pipeline stage — "New Lead" or "Opted In" — so they appear on your visual pipeline board and you can track their journey toward becoming a client.
  • Set the redirect. After submission, send the visitor to your thank-you page where they can access the resource.

Step 5: Build Your Thank-You Page

The thank-you page does double duty. First, it delivers what you promised — a download link, a video embed, or access instructions. Second, it introduces your next step. For a solo founder, this might be a booking calendar embed ("Ready to implement this? Book a free 15-minute strategy call"), a tripwire offer, or a video that pre-sells your service. Never waste the thank-you page on just "Thanks, check your email." The person is at peak engagement right after opting in. Use that attention.

Step 6: Set Up Automation

Navigate to GHL's Automation section and create a new workflow. Set the trigger to "Form Submitted" and select the form from your funnel. Now build the automation sequence:

  • Immediate email: Send the lead magnet delivery email with the download link. This fires within seconds of submission.
  • Wait 24 hours → Email 2: A follow-up that adds value. "Here are three ways to use the guide you downloaded yesterday." This reinforces engagement.
  • Wait 48 hours → Email 3: Introduce your offer. Share a case study or testimonial that bridges the free resource to your paid service. Include a link to your booking page or sales page.
  • Wait 48 hours → SMS: A short, personal text message. "Hey [First Name], did you get a chance to go through the guide? Happy to answer any questions." Text messages have 98% open rates. Use them.
  • Wait 72 hours → Email 4: Address the most common objection your prospects have. "Most consultants I work with worry about [objection]. Here is how we handle that."
  • Wait 72 hours → Email 5: Final nudge with urgency or scarcity if applicable. Invite them to book a call or make a purchase.

This entire sequence runs automatically for every person who enters your funnel. You build it once. It follows up with every lead for the next two weeks without you lifting a finger.

Step 7: Publish and Connect Your Domain

In the funnel settings, connect your custom domain so your funnel lives at yourdomain.com/free-guide instead of a generic GHL URL. Toggle the funnel to published. Test the full flow yourself — submit the form, check the CRM, confirm the automation triggers, verify the emails arrive. Then start driving traffic.

Key Elements of a High-Converting Funnel Page

The builder is just a tool. What you put on the page determines whether visitors convert or bounce. Here are the elements that consistently separate high-performing funnel pages from ones that collect dust.

A headline that states the outcome, not the offer. "Free Guide" is an offer. "How to Book 15 Discovery Calls Per Week Without Cold Outreach" is an outcome. Visitors care about what they will achieve, not what they will receive. Lead with the transformation.

A sub-headline that qualifies the reader. Not everyone should opt in. Your sub-headline should signal who this is for: "Built for B2B consultants billing $5K-$25K per project." This filters out poor-fit leads and makes qualified readers feel like the resource was built specifically for them.

Social proof above the fold. A testimonial, a client result, a number ("Used by 2,400+ solo consultants"), or a recognizable logo. Social proof reduces the psychological friction of giving up an email address. Even one line — "This guide helped me close my first $10K client in 3 weeks" — can measurably lift conversions.

A single, unmissable CTA. One button. One action. One color that contrasts with the rest of the page. Do not give visitors a navigation menu, a footer full of links, or multiple competing options. The page exists for one purpose. Everything on it should point toward the form.

Mobile optimization that is actually tested. Toggle GHL's mobile preview and review every element. Is the headline readable without zooming? Does the form render properly? Is the button large enough to tap with a thumb? Cut any elements that clutter the mobile experience. On mobile, simplicity converts.

Writing the actual copy for your funnel pages is where most solo founders get stuck. If you are staring at a blank headline field wondering how to phrase your offer, an AI writing tool like Jasper can accelerate the process significantly. Feed it your target audience, your offer details, and the outcome you deliver, and it will generate headline options, bullet points, and CTA text that you can refine in minutes instead of agonizing for hours. The goal is not to outsource your voice — it is to get a strong starting draft that you shape into something authentic.

Pipeline → email/SMS nurture sequence. Over the next 7 to 14 days, the contact receives pre-written emails and text messages that deliver value, build trust, and invite them to take the next step. You wrote the sequence once. It runs for every lead who enters.
