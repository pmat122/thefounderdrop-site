🚀 How Solo Founders Use a CRM to Scale

Week 1: Customer Capture

Solopreneurs start by importing existing customer data into their CRM. Set up basic customer fields: name, email, phone, company, deal status. Create a simple status pipeline: prospect, customer, repeat customer. You now have a single source of truth for all customer relationships instead of scattered emails and spreadsheets.

Week 2: Automation Setup

Build your first automation sequences. New customer gets onboarding email sequence. Follow-up email sends 3 days after sale. Monthly check-in goes to all customers. Abandoned cart recovery goes to prospects who viewed your offer but didn't buy. These automations run while you sleep, eliminating manual follow-up tasks.

Week 3: Pipeline Visibility

Your CRM now shows which prospects are moving forward and which are stuck. You see which customers are overdue for a check-in. Pipeline reviews take 10 minutes instead of 2 hours because data is organized. You're making decisions based on facts, not guesses.

Week 4: Revenue Impact

Completed automation sequences have led to repeat purchases. Forgotten prospects have been re-engaged. No-shows are down because appointment reminders are automated. Most solopreneurs see 20-30% revenue increase in the first month just from better follow-up alone.
