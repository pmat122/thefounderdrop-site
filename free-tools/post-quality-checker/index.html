How to Write Posts That Convert

1. Open a curiosity gap in the headline

Your headline must contain a specific number, timeframe, or outcome that makes the reader think "how?" or "what happened?" — generic headlines get scrolled past. See our social media tools guide for more.

2. Write 1,000+ words — longer captions convert better

Short captions attract scrollers. Long captions attract buyers. Follow the 7-beat story architecture: Lead, Pain, Discovery, Mechanism, Result, Insight, Gate.

3. End with a gated CTA, not a link

Use trigger words like PLAYBOOK or TOOLKIT to get comments. Comments boost reach. Links in captions get suppressed by the algorithm. Use a CRM with automated DM responses to deliver the resource instantly.

4. Automate the follow-up after comments

When someone comments your trigger word, you need to respond within seconds — not hours. Set up automated DM sequences that deliver the resource and capture the lead into your follow-up pipeline.
